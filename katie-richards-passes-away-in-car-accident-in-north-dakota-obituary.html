Katie Richards passes away in car accident in North Dakota: obituary

In a heart-wrenching incident, the North Dakota athletics community is mourning the untimely loss of Katie Richards, a former star in North Dakota women’s basketball. At the age of 45, Katie tragically lost her life in a devastating car accident on Thursday, November 2. Her passing has left her friends, coworkers, and the community in deep grief, remembering her incredible talent and dedication to the sport.

The tragic incident occurred while Katie was traveling south on 22nd Avenue NE in her 2021 Buick Enclave in Trail County, approximately eight miles west of Reynolds. Her car skidded off the road and overturned multiple times, resulting in disastrous consequences. Law enforcement officials quickly responded to the scene after the collision, which took place on November 2, 2023, around 12:10 a.m. Despite their efforts, Katie Richards tragically lost her life at the scene, leaving behind a legacy that will be remembered by all who knew her.

According to the North Dakota Highway Patrol, Katie was the only occupant in the car at the time of the collision. Investigators later discovered that she had not been wearing a seatbelt, underscoring the importance of adhering to safety regulations while operating a motor vehicle.

Katie Richards was not only an accomplished athlete at the University of North Dakota but also a beloved and respected member of Mayville State University. Her contributions to the sports industry were profound, particularly during the 1998–1999 women’s basketball season, where she showcased exceptional skills and unwavering commitment to the sport. Coach Gene Roebuck and her former teammates fondly remember her as a dedicated athlete and an amazing friend, emphasizing the impact she had on the basketball program.

As the community mourns the loss of Katie Richards, they cherish the memories of her extraordinary talent and the mark she left on the sports world.
